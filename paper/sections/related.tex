\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related}
%% ============================================================

\paragraph{Classical Invariant Generation.}
Abstract interpretation~\citep{cousot1977abstract} computes fixed-point
over-approximations in abstract domains but is restricted to the
chosen domain's expressiveness.
Template-based constraint
solving~\citep{colon2003linear,sankaranarayanan2004non} searches for
coefficients via LP/SDP, complete within the template family but
blind outside it.
The Houdini algorithm~\citep{flanagan2001houdini} iteratively removes
non-inductive conjuncts from a candidate set; we adopt it as
Stage~$F_4$ of our filter pipeline.
Daikon~\citep{daikon} and DIG~\citep{nguyen2012using} mine likely
invariants from traces but require a separate verifier pass.
ICE learning~\citep{garg2016learning} uses implication counterexamples
to guide decision-tree learners, achieving completeness within its
feature space.
All these methods are confined to their pre-designed search spaces.

\paragraph{LLM-Based Invariant Generation.}
Chakraborty and Lahiri~\citep{chakraborty2024towards} show GPT-4 can
generate invariants with a verification feedback loop.
Pei et al.~\citep{pei2023can} find iterative refinement essential for
Hoare-style annotations.
Kamath et al.~\citep{kamath2023finding} combine LLM generation with
bounded model checking.
Endres et al.~\citep{endres2024can} study whether LLMs can write
formal specifications in general, finding success on simpler
templates but frequent failures on complex loop invariants.
LLMs also suggest proof tactics~\citep{han2021proof,jiang2022draft} and
produce formal
proofs~\citep{wu2022autoformalization,lample2022hypertree,polu2022formal}.
A common thread is heavy reliance on external tool chains and the
strongest closed-source models, without exploring locally deployed
small models.

\paragraph{Learning-Based Loop Invariant Synthesis.}
Si et al.~\citep{si2018learning} frame invariant generation as a
supervised learning task, training a graph neural network on
programs from the SyGuS competition and using counterexample-guided
refinement.
Code2Inv~\citep{si2020code2inv} extends this with a continuous
representation of programs and invariants, using reinforcement
learning with the Z3 verifier as the reward signal.
CLN2INV~\citep{ryan2020cln2inv} learns continuous logic networks
that map program variables to invariant satisfaction scores,
achieving competitive performance on polynomial invariants.
These approaches pioneered neural invariant synthesis but are
constrained to fixed template families (e.g., octagonal or
polynomial) and do not leverage the flexible generation
capabilities of pretrained language models.

\paragraph{Mathematical Reasoning Distillation.}
Knowledge distillation for mathematical reasoning has attracted
significant attention.
The DeepSeek-R1 distilled models~\citep{deepseekr1} demonstrate that
distillation transfers reasoning trajectories to small models more
effectively than RL, which requires the student to rediscover them
through sparse rewards.
Mukherjee et al.~\citep{mukherjee2023orca} show that step-by-step
explanations from GPT-4 substantially improve small model reasoning
via imitation learning.
WizardMath~\citep{luo2023wizardmath} applies RLHF with process
supervision to enhance mathematical reasoning in open models.
Yuan et al.~\citep{yuan2023scaling} demonstrate that rejection
sampling from large models, combined with SFT on verified solutions,
offers a scalable alternative to RL for math.
MAmmoTH~\citep{yue2024mammoth} constructs instruction-tuning data
by combining CoT and program-of-thought rationales from GPT-4,
achieving strong math reasoning in 7B--70B models.
Our approach inherits the RLVR spirit but moves exploration entirely
offline, using formal verifier feedback to construct ground-truth
preference pairs for SFT\,+\,DPO distillation specifically for
the program verification domain.

\paragraph{Distillation vs.\ RL for Code and Reasoning.}
RLHF~\citep{ouyang2022training} and DPO~\citep{rafailov2023direct}
are standard LLM alignment techniques.
RLVR replaces human preferences with automated oracle rewards,
yielding large gains in math reasoning
(DeepSeek-R1~\citep{deepseekr1}, Tulu~3~\citep{lambert2024tulu}).
CodeRL~\citep{le2022coderl} and RLEF~\citep{gehring2024rlef}
apply RL with execution feedback for code generation.
However, binary verifier rewards are particularly sparse for
loop invariant generation: a single missing conjunct invalidates
the entire annotation.
This sparsity, combined with the reward-hackability of trivially
weak invariants, motivates our offline distillation approach over
online RL.

\paragraph{Training Data Synthesis.}
Existing benchmarks (NLA suite~\citep{svcomp},
linear set~\citep{si2018learning}) contain only dozens of hand-crafted
programs.
Grammar-based fuzzing~\citep{godefroid2008grammar} and probabilistic
synthesis~\citep{nori2015efficient} generate programs at scale but
lack semantic constraints.
\lf{} addresses this gap with semantic core templates
that guarantee well-defined invariant structure at tunable complexity.
