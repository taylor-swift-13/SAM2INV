\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
%% ============================================================

Loop invariant generation is a central challenge in program
verification.
Traditional
approaches~\citep{cousot1977abstract,colon2003linear,flanagan2001houdini,daikon,nguyen2012using}
handle specific loop families but generalise poorly and incur
prohibitive analysis time.
Recent LLM-based
methods~\citep{chakraborty2024towards,pei2023can,kamath2023finding}
bring fresh perspectives, yet universally rely on extensive SMT
solving, complex multi-round workflows, and the most powerful
closed-source models---revealing that the task remains exceptionally
hard even for LLMs, demanding \emph{logical reasoning},
\emph{program comprehension}, and \emph{formal annotation mastery}
simultaneously.

The heavy dependence on closed-source APIs creates deployment
bottlenecks: a single project may contain hundreds of loops, each
requiring multiple sampling-and-verification rounds, incurring
prohibitive token costs and latency.
API uncontrollability---model updates, service interruptions, data
privacy---further limits safety-critical deployment.
Training a \emph{locally deployable} small model is therefore of
considerable practical value.

We focus on \emph{numerical} loop invariants---linear or polynomial
inequalities over program variables whose verification is
SMT-automatable.
This preserves sufficient reasoning difficulty while keeping the
formalisation barrier low, offering a unique lens to examine small
models' integrated mathematical and programming abilities.

Realising this goal faces four challenges:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{Data scarcity.}
  Existing annotated corpora are extremely limited and carry
  data-leakage risks.
\item \textbf{Sparse feedback.}
  The SMT verifier yields a binary pass/fail verdict; hand-crafted
  reward shaping introduces human-prior bias and alignment risk.
\item \textbf{Reward hackability.}
  Trivially weak annotations
  (e.g.\ \lstinline|loop invariant \true;|) pass the check
  yet capture no program property.
\item \textbf{Base-model bottleneck.}
  GRPO-style RL~\citep{deepseekr1} requires multiple correct
  rollouts, but weak models rarely produce any within a practical
  sampling budget.
\end{enumerate}

These constraints favour \emph{knowledge distillation} over
RL~\citep{deepseekr1}: distillation transfers the teacher's reasoning
trajectories directly, bypassing the autonomous exploration
bottleneck.
We choose GPT-5-nano as the teacher---bridging the student--teacher
gap~\citep{deepseekr1} while keeping per-program token cost tractable.

To address data scarcity, we build \lf{}, a DSL-based
generator that synthesises diverse numerical loop programs with known
semantic structure, eliminating data leakage by construction
(Section~\ref{sec:loop-factory}).
We then construct a unified \emph{sampling-and-filtering pipeline}
(Section~\ref{sec:overview}) that serves both training data
preparation and test-time inference, differing only in the scale of
parallel teacher sampling.
The pipeline decomposes candidates into conjuncts, filters them
through syntax, trace-semantic, deduplication, Houdini, and
triviality stages, and synthesises reverse chains-of-thought for the
certified output.
Survivors form \emph{chosen} samples; rejects become \emph{rejected}
samples for DPO.
Two augmentation strategies---off-policy conjunct recombination
and on-policy student negatives---further enrich the preference
signal.
A two-stage SFT\,+\,DPO recipe completes the distillation
(Section~\ref{sec:method}).

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{\lf{}}: a probabilistic DSL for
  numerical loop synthesis overcoming data scarcity and leakage
  (Section~\ref{sec:loop-factory}).
\item \textbf{A multi-stage sampling-and-filtering pipeline}
  integrating verifier feedback, semantic deduplication, trivial
  invariant filtering, and reverse CoT synthesis to produce certified
  annotations (Section~\ref{sec:method}).
\item \textbf{An offline distillation strategy} with on-policy
  augmentation, producing SFT\,+\,DPO training data grounded in
  formal verification without online RL
  (Section~\ref{sec:offline}).
\end{enumerate}
