%% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
%% ============================================================

We have presented \textsc{SAM2INV}, a system for certified loop
invariant generation built on three contributions.

\textsc{LoopFactory}, our probabilistic DSL, overcomes the scarcity of
annotated loop programs by synthesising structured numeric loops with
freely tunable complexity, supplying both training inputs and controlled
benchmarks.

The \emph{three-stage sampling-and-filtering pipeline} assembles a
diverse candidate pool through smart tiered sampling and parallel LLM
generation, then eliminates incorrect candidates via an ACSL syntax
filter, an execution-trace semantic filter, and Houdini formal pruning.
Every surviving invariant is formally certified by Frama-C/WP.
The pipeline delivers substantial improvements even before fine-tuning:
union assembly discovers conjuncts no single call would produce, and
the lightweight filters prevent parse failures from reaching the
verifier.

The \emph{offline knowledge distillation} strategy moves GRPO-style
exploration into data preparation: a teacher model generates candidates,
the verifier filters them, and the resulting chosen/rejected pairs form
SFT and DPO training data grounded entirely in formal verification---no
human annotation, no online reward model, and no reward hacking.
Two-stage SFT\,+\,DPO fine-tuning transfers the teacher's exploration
benefit to a smaller student model while the pipeline guarantees
soundness throughout the improvement cycle.

Several directions remain for future work.
Most directly, systematic fine-tuning experiments should measure how
the success rate improves as the student model is updated over
successive training rounds.
Extending the system to heap-manipulating programs, where invariants
must express separation-logic properties, is a natural next step; our
codebase already partially supports Coq/VST verification.
Finally, integrating symbolic techniques---such as abstract
interpretation for initial bounds or interpolation for strengthening
failing candidates---could yield a hybrid symbolic--neural architecture
with stronger guarantees outside the current system's success region.
