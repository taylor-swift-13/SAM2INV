\section{Numerical Loop Synthesis}\label{sec:loop-factory}
%% ============================================================

To overcome the scarcity of annotated numeric loop programs, we
design \textsc{LoopFactory}, a probabilistic DSL that synthesises
well-formed C loop programs at arbitrary scale.
A program has the form $\textbf{Init}; \textbf{Loop}^+$, where loops
may be sequential or nested; structural complexity is freely tunable
via fuel parameters governing the number of variables, loops,
assignments, and conditional blocks per program.
Because uniform sampling from the grammar overwhelmingly produces
divergent or semantically vacuous programs, we constrain generation
with \emph{semantic core templates}: each template fixes a
termination-guaranteed update skeleton and a known invariant shape,
while leaving variable names and constants to probabilistic
instantiation.
The 60 templates span eight proof-obligation families---linear
conservation, monotone accumulation, proportional stride, modular
counting, state-machine transitions, division/GCD, multiplicative
redistribution, and polynomial recurrence---ensuring that the corpus
covers the full range of arithmetic loop patterns an invariant-generation
policy must handle.
Using \textsc{LoopFactory} we synthesise 2{,}000 programs; 1{,}900
are used for training and 100 are held out as a synthetic test set.
Full DSL definition, template descriptions, and generation examples
are given in Appendix~\ref{app:loop-factory}.
