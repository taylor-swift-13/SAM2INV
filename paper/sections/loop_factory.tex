\section{Numerical Loop Synthesis}\label{sec:loop-factory}
%% ============================================================

To overcome the scarcity of annotated numeric loop programs, we
design \lf{}, a probabilistic DSL that synthesises
well-formed C loop programs at arbitrary scale.
A program has the form $\textbf{Init}; \textbf{Loop}^+$, where loops
may be sequential or nested; structural complexity is freely tunable
via fuel parameters governing the number of variables, loops,
assignments, and conditional blocks per program.
Because uniform sampling from the grammar overwhelmingly produces
divergent or semantically vacuous programs, we constrain generation
with \emph{semantic core templates}: each template fixes a
termination-guaranteed update skeleton and a known invariant shape,
while leaving variable names and constants to probabilistic
instantiation.

To increase diversity within each semantic template, we optionally
use a constrained LLM generation step during instantiation.
The model proposes a loop guard and update fragment according to
specific semantic requirements; invalid outputs are rejected and
generation falls back to the original DSL rule.

Table~\ref{tab:semantic-families} summarises the eight proof-obligation
families covered by the 60 templates, ensuring that the corpus spans
the full range of arithmetic loop patterns an invariant-generation
policy must handle.

\begin{table}[t]
\caption{Semantic families of \lf{} templates. Each family encodes a
distinct proof obligation pattern with guaranteed termination.}
\label{tab:semantic-families}
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Family} & \textbf{Invariant Pattern} & \textbf{Example} \\
\midrule
Linear conservation      & $a + b = N$                       & transfer loops \\
Monotone accumulation    & $s \mathrel{+}= f(k)$            & prefix sums \\
Proportional stride      & $j = c \cdot i$                   & scaled counters \\
Modular counting         & $c \equiv t \pmod{T}$             & cyclic counters \\
State-machine transition & finite-state updates              & flag toggles \\
Division / GCD           & $a = q \cdot d + r$               & Euclidean division \\
Multiplicative redistr.  & $z + xy = a_0 b_0$                & Russian multiply \\
Polynomial recurrence    & $zw \ge p^2$                      & Cauchy--Schwarz \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Using \lf{} we synthesise 2{,}000 programs; 1{,}900
are used for training and 100 are held out as a synthetic test set.
Full DSL definition, template descriptions, and generation examples
are given in Appendix~\ref{app:loop-factory}.
